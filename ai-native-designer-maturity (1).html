The AI-Native Designer,
in two muscles.

“AI-native” gets used as one thing. It’s really two — and they grow independently. One is about how you work. The other is about what you ship.

Designing with AI

AI as your design partner. This is your craft and workflow — how a brief becomes a result.

Designing for AI

AI as the product. This is what reaches the user — the interaction, the trust, the loop.

Two ladders. Read both, then take the quiz.
Ladder A · Your workflow

Designing with AI

From asking a chatbot for help → to running a system that designs alongside you.

L1
Level 1Dabbler“The Tourist”

One-off asks in a chat window. Single-turn, reactive, starts from zero every session.

Spot itNo saved context or setup. Output quality swings wildly because the model never has the background.
ExamplePastes a screen in, asks “improve the copy,” ships whatever comes back.
L2
Level 2Operator“The Regular”

Uses AI consistently for recurring tasks and writes constrained prompts. Still hand-drives every step.

Spot itA few reused go-to prompts, can iterate on tone — but it’s all manual, one task at a time.
ExampleReliably drafts empty states, microcopy and first-pass flows before refining in Figma.
L3
Level 3Fluent“The Pilot”

Sets the AI up before working — a living context file (brief, audience, voice, system rules) — and prototypes with a real model, not static mockups.

Spot itKeeps a context doc; chains steps; evaluates output instead of accepting it. The honest test: would you feel the loss if AI were removed?
ExampleBuilds a live-model prototype of a creator-discovery flow, sees responses take ~6s, and designs the latency state around real output.
L4
Level 4Orchestrator“The Conductor”

Designs the workflow itself. Delegates multi-step work to agents and builds reusable scaffolding — skills, prompt-specs, MCP — then spends energy on direction and the quality bar.

Spot itThe conductor: hands the AI the right context, components and criteria, then judges the result. Builds assets the team reuses.
ExampleShips a reusable “TIST screen generator” skill — tokens, voice and component rules baked in — that the studio runs, then curates.
How to measure Ladder A
  1. Don’t trust self-ratings — watch behaviour. What share of work touches AI and could be handed off without watching every output?
  2. Persistent context, or a cold start every time?
  3. Do they produce reusable assets others adopt?
Ladder B · Your product

Designing for AI

From bolting on a chatbot → to designing the whole intelligent loop the user lives in.

L1
Level 1Bolt-on“The Magician”

Treats AI as a feature to add (“put a chatbot on it”). Designs only the happy path and the magic demo.

Spot itThe flow assumes AI always works. Onboarding over-promises “magic.” No failure states.
ExampleA “Magic Fix” button that returns a flawless result in the mock — with no thought to when it doesn’t.
L2
Level 2Pattern-aware“The Matchmaker”

Matches the interaction pattern to the task — automate vs. augment vs. converse — instead of chatbot-for-everything.

Spot itCan justify why a feature is a background agent vs. a chat vs. an inline suggestion.
ExampleBooking a slot = a silent background agent. Vetting a creator’s brand-fit = AI flags anomalies for a human to decide.
L3
Level 3Trust & failure designer“The Diplomat”

Designs for the messy reality of models: graceful failure, honest confidence, disclosure, latency and variance.

Spot itEvery AI surface has a fallback. Copy communicates limits. The bot is clearly labelled a bot.
Example“Here’s who we found” + a manual fix instead of an error. Support chat discloses it’s automated and sells the benefit, not the tech.
L4
Level 4Systems & feedback designer“The Architect”

Designs the full loop: feedback signals that teach the system, the right success metrics, and agents as a second kind of “user.”

Spot itSuccess is a correction / edit rate, not a vanity count. Explicit and implicit feedback are built into the UI.
ExampleAn AI campaign-brief generator measured by edit distance; a skip-within-10s wired as a negative signal that visibly updates recommendations.
How to measure Ladder B
  1. Failure and fallback states, or only the happy path?
  2. Is the AI honestly disclosed?
  3. Is there a feedback loop that improves the system?
  4. What single metric defines success? (Edit / correction rate beats “engagement.”)
